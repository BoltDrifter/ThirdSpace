# 📄 Product Requirements Document: ThirdSpace (v1.0)

**Project Lead:** Gemini & Founder  
**Status:** Draft / Conceptual  
**Priority:** Moderate (Social Crisis Responsive)

---

## 1. Executive Summary
**ThirdSpace** is a real-time "Social Gravity" map designed to combat the loneliness epidemic. Unlike static maps (Google Maps) or event boards (Meetup), ThirdSpace shows **where the people are right now.** It facilitates low-stakes, non-transactional human interaction for remote workers and urban transplants.

## 2. Target Audience
* **The "Digital Nomad" & Remote Worker:** Needs a place to work that isn't their kitchen table but offers social "background noise."
* **The "New-to-City" Expat:** Looking for low-friction ways to meet people without the pressure of a 1-on-1 "friend date."
* **The "AI-Fatigued" Gen Z:** Craving physical presence and authentic "vibes" over curated social media feeds.

---

## 3. Core Features (The MVP Scope)

### 3.1 The "Social Gravity" Map
* **Real-Time Heatmap:** Visual representation of activity. Areas glow brighter based on "Check-ins" and "Active Beacons."
* **Vibe Tags:** Venues aren't categorized by "Food/Drink," but by atmosphere: *#DeepWork, #SocialBuzz, #CreativeFlow, #QuietContemplation.*
* **Live Occupancy:** Integration with venue APIs or user-reported data to show "Crowd Density."

### 3.2 "The Beacon" (User-Generated Meetups)
* **Drop a Pin:** Users can host a spontaneous event (e.g., "I'm reading at the park, come say hi").
* **Capacity Limits:** Creators set a "Table Size" (e.g., 2–6 people) to keep it intimate and low-stakes.
* **Auto-Expiration:** Beacons vanish after 3 hours to ensure map accuracy.

### 3.3 The "Vibe Check" (Proof of Presence)
* **Proximity Check-In:** Users must be within 50 meters to "Check-in" and update the venue's status.
* **Status Updates:** Users can leave 140-character "Status Pulses" (e.g., *"Great playlist at Cafe Nero right now, lots of open outlets"*).

---

## 4. Monetization Modules (The "Circle" Model)

### 4.1 B2B: Venue Partner Dashboard
* **The "Boost" Button:** Venues pay to increase their "Gravity" glow on the map during off-peak hours.
* **Analytics:** Insights into the demographics and "Vibe" preferences of people nearby.

### 4.2 B2C: The "Citizen" Subscription
* **Priority Beacons:** Subscribers’ events stay at the top of the "Active Now" list.
* **The "Community Pass":** Discounted entry or "Reserved Seating" at partner ThirdSpaces.

---

## 5. Technical Stack (The 2-Month Build)
* **Frontend:** React Native (Cross-platform iOS/Android) for rapid deployment.
* **Backend:** Firebase or Supabase (Real-time database and Auth).
* **Mapping:** Mapbox SDK (More customizable and "social" aesthetic than Google Maps).
* **Location:** Geofencing for "Presence Verification."

---

## 6. Success Metrics (KPIs)
* **Stickiness (DAU/MAU):** How often users check the map before leaving their house.
* **Conversion to Physical:** Ratio of "Map Views" to "Physical Check-ins."
* **Beacon Density:** Average number of user-created events per square mile in the Alpha zone.

---

## 7. Safety & Community Standards
* **The "Handshake" System:** Users must request to join a Beacon; the host accepts based on a minimal profile.
* **Report & Ghosting Logic:** Users who frequently "Ghost" (sign up and don't show) lose the ability to join Beacons for 24 hours.
* **Zero-Algorithm Feed:** The map is sorted by **Distance** and **Time**, not by an engagement algorithm.

---

## 8. 8-Week Launch Timeline

| Week | Milestone | Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-2** | **Logic & Wireframes** | Map UI design and User Flow for "Lighting a Beacon." |
| **3-4** | **The Alpha Map** | Integrating Mapbox and Geofencing for Check-ins. |
| **5-6** | **The Social Layer** | Implementing User Profiles, Messaging, and the Beacon Timer. |
| **7** | **Beta Testing** | Soft launch in **one specific neighborhood** (e.g., Williamsburg or Shoreditch). |
| **8** | **Full Launch** | Opening the "Venue Boost" dashboard for local cafe partners. |

---

> **The "Anti-Ghosting" Rule:** If a user "lights a Beacon" but leaves the geofenced area for more than 10 minutes, the Beacon is automatically extinguished. This maintains the "Real-Time" integrity of the map.
